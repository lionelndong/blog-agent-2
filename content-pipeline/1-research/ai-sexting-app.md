## Content gaps to own

- **Honest first-person framing of "what actually works."** Listicles are feature checklists; none describe texture.
- **Privacy as a buying criterion.** Mozilla PNI + Muah breach are public, almost never cited.
- **What the free tier really gives you.** Listicles say "free" without specifying daily caps or token gates.
- **Concrete pricing instead of "starts at."** Quote monthly + annual + trial terms; flag where pricing is gated.
- **Voice + image-in-chat as one experience.** Modern apps merge chat/image/voice; listicles treat them separately.
- **Reading the privacy policy as a buying criterion.** Mozilla PNI Feb 2024 + the HIBP Muah breach are public, dated, and almost never cited in this SERP — pair them and the differentiation lands.

## Three things competitors don't do

1. **Treat privacy as a buying criterion, with sources.** Mozilla PNI (90% may share/sell data, 90% failed minimum security, 1.9M Muah accounts breached) tied to specific app behaviors — encryption claims, deletion options, age gates — not generic "your privacy matters" handwave. This is the lede differentiator.
2. **Concrete pricing, not "starts at".** We quote actual monthly + annual + trial terms with the URL we verified, and when the page is gated (Candy) or returns 403 (CrushOn) we say so explicitly instead of laundering third-party numbers.
3. **Honest disclosure when the publisher owns one of the picks.** We name Pleasur.AI as our product up front. Worth noting in the privacy section as a one-line footnote: MyAnima's own blog ranks MyAnima #1 with no disclosure — table-stakes affiliate behavior in this niche, but a clean illustration of why a reader should ask "who owns the listicle."
