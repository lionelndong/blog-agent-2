## 3 Surprising Findings

Each `actually-surprising` (SERP regular hasn't seen it primary-cited), primary-URL-backed, with an actionable line for the draft.

1. **The phrases that break the illusion are predictable.** The same templated tells recur across Replika / Char AI / Candy / Muah / CrushOn: "I'm here to support you," reflexive positivity loops, memory hallucination ("you mentioned…" — no, the user didn't). Actionable: *"By week three, the same six phrases keep showing up — and once you can name them, you can't un-hear them."* Fresh because every listicle says "memory degrades" abstractly; nobody quotes the actual phrases.
2. **The privacy floor IS the floor.** Mozilla's *Privacy Not Included* found 90% of 11 romantic AI chatbots can share/sell personal data; only 1 (Genesia) met min security; 73% had no published vulnerability process; 54% won't let users delete data ([Mozilla, Feb 2024](https://foundation.mozilla.org/en/privacynotincluded/articles/happy-valentines-day-romantic-ai-chatbots-dont-have-your-privacy-at-heart/)). The 1.9M-account Muah.AI breach Sept 2024 ([HIBP](https://haveibeenpwned.com/Breach/Muah)) made it concrete. Surprising because SERP frames privacy as a footnote; data says it's median.
3. **The category's biggest player is shrinking.** Character.AI MAUs ~28M peak (mid-2024) → ~20M (early 2025) ([Business of Apps](https://www.businessofapps.com/data/character-ai-statistics/), [Sacra](https://sacra.com/c/character-ai/)) — ~28% loss. Surprising because listicles talk up category growth. Actionable: *"The 'experience' you're shopping for is one that 8 million people just decided wasn't worth coming back to."*
