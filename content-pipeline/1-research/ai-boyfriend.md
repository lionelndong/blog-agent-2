### Key community signals

**The gender gap is closing faster than the SERP reflects.** "AI girlfriend" searches still outnumber "AI boyfriend" 9:1, but female interest is the fastest-growing segment. China is the proof case: women aged 25–40 are the *primary* users of apps like Xingye (500K DAU), driven by social pressures around marriage. The US is following with a lag.

**Memory is the #1 feature users actually care about.** The feature that separates "I use this daily" from "I deleted it after a week" is whether the AI remembers past conversations. Nomi AI and Sidekicks win loyalty specifically on memory continuity — it matters more than image gen, voice quality, or customization depth.

**Privacy is an underreported crisis.** In October 2025, two AI companion apps leaked 43 million intimate messages and 600,000 photos from 400,000+ users. In February 2026, another app exposed 300 million messages from 25 million users. Mozilla research found more than half of AI companion apps have significant security vulnerabilities. Yet almost no top-ranking "best AI boyfriend" article covers this meaningfully — it's mentioned in passing FAQ sections at most.

**Voice calls are the new battleground.** As of May 2026, only five AI companion apps have working voice calls: Candy AI, Nectar AI, Nomi AI, Replika, and Kindroid. Candy AI's Live Call (launched February 2026) leads with sub-1.5-second latency and persistent memory across calls at $12.99/month. This is where platforms are competing for differentiation.

**Harvard research validates the emotional value.** Harvard researchers found AI companions can reduce loneliness about as effectively as talking to a real person — the key variable being whether users felt genuinely heard. This counters the dismissive "it's just a chatbot" narrative that dominates mainstream coverage.
