## The seven decisions you make before opening the creator

Every build is the same seven decisions in the same order. Write your answers down on paper before you open the creator and you cut your regeneration count by an order of magnitude.

**Art style.** Realistic or anime. Pick this first because every other decision renders differently inside it. Don't switch mid-build; you'll lose your settings.

**Archetype.** The one-line description of your character — "athletic surfer," "soft librarian," "club promoter," "academic in oversized sweaters." Two or three words. This is the anchor every other decision hangs on.

**Ethnicity.** Six standard presets across the category: East Asian, South Asian, Latina, Black, White, Mixed. Pick the one that matches your archetype, not the one that catches your eye in the dropdown.

**Hair.** Color and length. Match your archetype, not the style trend.

**Body type.** Six recognizable presets — slim, curvy, athletic, hourglass, plus, petite. Each one outputs a specific look that the next decision either supports or fights.

**Breast and butt sliders.** A continuous range layered on top of the body type. Where most builds break is here. The slider direction often fights the body-type preset; we'll get to the fix shortly.

**Outfit.** Last, because it's the most disposable. You can change the outfit inside chat by asking the companion to send an image in different clothes. The body cannot be changed the same way.

## The breast and butt sliders (the part most guides skip)

Breast and butt sliders are the body decision the SERP avoids naming. Of the six product pages with public marketing copy, three name the breast slider; two name the butt slider [link]. Candy.AI's copy leaves both unnamed.

So this section names what they do, where they break, and the prompt phrasing that fixes the most common failure mode.

The sliders output a continuous range, not three buckets. Push either slider near its maximum and the output reads as edited rather than rendered: bolted-on proportions, oddly drawn shadows, a waist that doesn't connect to the hips. Pull it near the minimum and the body type stops matching its preset.

That's a model-architecture limit, not a Pleasur.AI quirk. Every realistic builder in the category breaks the same way at the ends of the range.

The combination failure mode is where most regenerations get burned. Pair "athletic" body type with a maxed breast slider and the model fights itself.

Visible muscle definition and high-volume soft tissue read as anatomically inconsistent. The model resolves the conflict by producing something that looks edited rather than rendered.

Same story for "petite" plus a maxed butt slider. The output looks wrong — you don't quite know why, you regenerate ten times, and nothing improves.

The fix is mechanical. Leave the slider near the middle of the range. If you want a high-volume look, pick the body-type preset that already implies it — curvy or hourglass — and let the preset do the work the slider was overshooting.
