# Adversarial read — how-to-make-an-ai-girlfriend

Skeptical industry expert, 400-word ceiling. No fixes proposed. One genuine positive at the end.

## 1. The DIY recipe is gestures, not a recipe
The H2 promises "a real 2026 recipe (not the 2022 one that ranks)." What's delivered is a list of four component names (Llama-3-8B + LoRA, ElevenLabs, SDXL, Gradio) and a Vast.ai spot price. There is no command, no repo, no concrete LoRA config, no example training step, no quantization note, no hint at dataset format. A DIY reader who actually wants to build this gets less than the 2022 tutorial it ridicules. "A reasonable rhythm: weekend one gets you a model that responds in character" is filler. How? On what data?

## 2. "Memory is the thing every guide skips" is a thesis that pays out in one paragraph
The piece teases memory as the differentiator, then resolves it with "add a vector store, re-rank, raise k." That's the same shallow gesture it criticized in the 2022 tutorial. No mention of embedding model choice, summarization cadence, context-window tradeoffs, eval. The retrieval pipeline is the one part the article promised to be specific about and it goes abstract on cue.

## 3. The pricing table is a covert ad with a fig leaf
Five rows, four of them gated or third-party-sourced, and Pleasur.AI is the only row with a working pricing page link. The disclaimer ("five rows, all third-party sourced, no row left looking like a covert ad") draws attention to the exact thing it's trying to deflect. The Pleasur.AI row also slips in a price range ("$13.99–$27.99 across regions") that no other row gets — the others are flat monthly numbers. Inconsistent.

## 4. The Replika anecdote does double-duty and earns its keep at neither station
It's cited twice — once in DIY for "personality drift," once in week-three failure — with the same Vice link and nearly identical framing. Reusing the same anecdote in two H2s in a 3,000-word piece is a structural tell.

## 5. The Stanford ethics paragraph is a non-sequitur
Loneliness stats land 80% through a piece that has been about engineering tradeoffs. Two sentences after, the piece pivots to "hour-one is easy, week-three is the work." The ethics nod reads like a guilt-shield, not an integrated point.

## What works
The pre-flight six-dimension comparison ("Time to first working version… DIY vs off-the-shelf") is genuinely useful. Concrete dimensions, concrete answers, a real filter for self-selection. That section justifies the whole framing.
