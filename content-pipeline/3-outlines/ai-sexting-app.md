## Privacy is a real buying criterion (read these two before you sign up)

**Target:** ~280 words

- **BLUF:** Two public, dated, almost-never-cited sources should decide whether you trust any AI sexting app you sign up for — Mozilla's *Privacy Not Included* romantic-chatbot report (Feb 2024) and the HIBP record for the Muah.AI breach (Sept 2024).
- **Key points:**
  - **[Mozilla *Privacy Not Included*, Feb 14 2024](https://www.mozillafoundation.org/en/privacynotincluded/articles/happy-valentines-day-romantic-ai-chatbots-dont-have-your-privacy-at-heart/):** of 11 romantic AI chatbots tested, 90% may share or sell personal data; 90% failed minimum security standards (only Genesia AI Friend & Partner passed); 73% have not published a vulnerability disclosure process; 64% lack clear encryption info; 54% allow users to delete personal data. All 11 earned the *Privacy Not Included warning label.
  - **[HIBP — Muah.AI](https://haveibeenpwned.com/PwnedWebsites), Sept 2024:** 1.9 million accounts exposed; added to HIBP Oct 8, 2024. The concrete cost of the Mozilla findings showing up in the real world.
  - **What to actually check before you sign up:** is there a published deletion option? Is the privacy policy linked from the footer of the live site? Is there a payment path that doesn't tie to your real name (crypto / virtual card)? Is the signup tied to your primary email or can you use a forwarder?
